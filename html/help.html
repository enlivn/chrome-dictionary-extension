popupDic is designed to help foreign language learners better remember new vocabulary they acquire online. The idea behind it is very simple: repetition and context are key to remembering new words that we learn, especially when they are words in a foreign language.

Although there are a number of excellent dictionary websites online, one major problem is that once we have looked up a word and then moved on to a new page, we usually tend to forget the word we looked up. A number of times, you might find yourself looking up the same word from different webpages and only remembering that you have actually looked the word up before after you have checked up on its meaning. This can be frustrating. popupDic aims to solve this problem by:

  • Maintaining an easily-accessible list of words that you have looked up. Although you could use an external program like a Word processor or flashcard software to do this, popupDic gives you the word list functionality right in your browser, just two clicks away at all times!
  • Saving the context of words (the sentence they were found in) when you look them up. The context of the word is often a very good memory aid.
  • Highlighting on any page the words you have looked up (even if they were looked up from other pages). This means you can challenge yourself to remember what the words mean. And if you don't remember, popupDic can always remind you! ;)

What's more, popupDic does all this without disturbing your normal browsing experience. You turn on the features you want, when you want it.

popupDic is currently aimed at individuals learning French.

popupDic uses the excellent Wordreference API. Many thanks to the folks at Wordreference for providing such a wonderful resource free of charge.

A quick look at the extension icon will tell you whether popupDic is on or off, as the following image shows (see part enclosed in blue rectangle). If it says 'ON', the extension is active.

If it says 'OFF', you need to turn the extension on. To do so, click on the icon and toggle the status button, as shown in the following figure (see parts enclosed in blue rectangles).

You need to do this only ONCE for every browsing session (until the browser is closed and reopened). Turning off the extension is equally easy. Simply toggle the Status button again.

Make sure popupDic is turned ON first. Hover over 'Turning popupDic On/Off' above if you're not sure about how to do this.

To look up word meanings, hold down the 'Alt' key on your keyboard and double-click the word you wish to look up. The meaning of the word pops up on the page, as shown in the figure below. It's as simple as that!

Click outside the popup or on the 'cross' icon to close the popup.

Make sure popupDic is turned ON first. Hover over 'Turning popupDic On/Off' above if you're not sure about how to do this.

The sidebar displays a list of words you have looked up before. The sidebar is not displayed by default. To turn it on, toggle the 'Sidebar' switch after clicking on the extension icon, as the image below shows (see parts enclosed in blue rectangles).

If the page is refreshed, the sidebar will turn off and you must turn it back on again. Sidebar's must also be toggled individually for each webpage. This is so that it interferes as little as possible with your normal browsing.

The sidebar also saves the context the word was found in as a gloss (memory aid). You can see the context by clicking on the bulb icon, as the following image shows (see part enclosed in blue rectangles). Clicking on the bulb again will hide the gloss.

'Clear List' will clear all words from the extension's memory. Be careful as this cannot be undone!

Make sure popupDic is turned ON first. Hover over 'Turning popupDic On/Off' above if you're not sure about how to do this.

Highlighting will help you exercise your memory. Turn it on/off by toggling the 'Highlighting' button after clicking on the extension icon, as the image below shows (see part enclosed in blue rectangles).

Like the sidebar, highlighting must be turned on and off individually for each page. Additionally, refreshing the page will remove the highlighting and you must turn it on again if you want it.

When Highlighting is on, words you have looked up before (on any page) will be highlighted on the current page. You can check if you remember what they mean.

Hovering over a word shows a popup that asks precisely this question.

  • If you do remember the meaning, simply move the mouse away and the popup will disappear.
  • If you don't remember the meaning, clicking on 'No' will display it for you, as the image below shows. You can then close the popup by clicking on the 'Close' icon or anywhere outside the popup.
